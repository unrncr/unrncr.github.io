Neo Cortical Repository and Reports

Making Computational Neuroscience more accessible

Our Project

The Neo Cortical Repository and Reports (NCR) team aims to create an intuitive interface for understanding the brain simulation data. NCR is a portion of a greater web based application for computational neuroscientists. The web application is designed to make creating brain models and simulations easier and more intuitive for members of the neuroscience community. The Reports tab of the application graphically shows the simulation data in an understandable format. The repository tab is an interface to NCR's brain model database. The database gives researchers the opportunity to host and find a variety of models. Overall, the goal of the project is to make computational neuroscience more accessible to researchers world wide.

Brain Computation Lab

The NCR team works closely with students and faculty from the University of Nevada's Brain Computation Laboratory. The lab is dedicated to research in the field of computational neuroscience. The research includes many fields such as Computer Science and Egineering, Medicine, Physiology and Cell Biology, and Biomedical Engineering. All of these interesting fields come together to increase understanding of artificial brain activity.

University of Nevada

The Neo Cortical Repository and Reports (NCR) team is a group of undergraduate students from the University of Nevada Computer Science and Engineering program. The team is advised by faculty of the university and given the opportunity to be part of an impactful project. The project is part of CS 426, Senior Project in Computer Science in the Spring of 2014.

The class requires presentations and deliverables from each project team. The final workshop for the class is May 2, 2014. At the workshop, the students will present their projects and discuss the course's design process. For more information, visit the course website.
